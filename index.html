#8 Early Risk-Off / Defensive Rotation

Investors are reducing risk gradually — not panicking, but rotating toward safety. The sequence matters: small caps weaken first, growth weakens next, and institutions continue holding core large-cap equities as the last line of defense. This is a deterioration-in-progress regime. It often precedes broader weakness if the ES pillar eventually cracks. The market is telling you that credit-sensitive and speculative segments are already under stress, even while the S&P 500 looks relatively composed. A classic "calm before the storm" pattern.
